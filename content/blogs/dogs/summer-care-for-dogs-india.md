# Summer Care for Dogs in India: Daily Routine for Hot Cities

Hot months in India can be tough on dogs, especially in cities with concrete, traffic heat, and humidity. This guide on dog summer care India offers a calm, practical routine for normal homes and apartments. The focus is simple: keep your dog cool, hydrated, and comfortable without overdoing it.

## Quick answer: dog summer care India basics
Keep walks early and late, avoid hot pavement, offer clean water often, and keep meals light. Limit sun exposure during peak heat and watch for overheating. A steady, cool routine is safer than sudden changes.

## Why summer is harder for dogs in Indian cities
Indian summers bring high temperatures, humid nights, and heat stored in concrete buildings. Dogs in apartments face limited airflow and hotter floors. Even short outdoor trips can feel intense because roads and tiles retain heat.

## Daily routine for hot Indian cities
### Morning (before 8 am)
- Take the main walk early, before the sun gets strong.
- Choose shaded routes with grass or dirt when possible.
- Offer fresh water as soon as you return.

### Afternoon (12 pm to 4 pm)
- Avoid outdoor activity during peak heat.
- Keep your dog in the coolest room with a fan and good airflow.
- Provide a clean water bowl and check it often.

### Evening (after 6 pm)
- Do the second walk when the ground cools down.
- Keep the walk short if the air is still hot or humid.
- Allow rest after the walk and avoid heavy play.

### Night (after 9 pm)
- Keep sleeping areas ventilated and comfortable.
- Offer water nearby so your dog can drink easily.
- If your dog seems restless, move them to a cooler spot.

## Pavement heat guidance
Hot roads and tiles can burn paws. A simple check is to place your palm on the ground for a few seconds. If it feels too hot for you, it is too hot for your dog. Stick to shaded lanes, parks, or grassy patches.

## Hydration and cooling tips for Indian homes
- Keep multiple bowls of water in different rooms.
- Use a damp cloth to wipe paws and belly after walks.
- Place a cool mat or a thin cotton sheet in the resting area.
- Avoid ice-cold water; room temperature water is easier to handle.

## Heat safety without fear
Overheating can happen quickly in summer. Keep activity low during peak heat and focus on rest. If your dog seems unusually tired, drools heavily, or refuses water, move them to a cooler spot and contact a vet for guidance.

## Practical home habits that help
- Keep curtains drawn during the hottest hours.
- Open windows for airflow when it is cooler outside.
- Avoid leaving dogs in balconies or sunlit spots.
- Skip heavy grooming in peak heat, but keep fur clean and dry.

## What PawVeda recommends

- Treat pavement heat as a real risk and plan routes around it.
- Keep hydration simple and frequent.
- Use common-sense cooling in Indian homes rather than extreme measures.

## FAQs
### How many walks are safe in summer?
Two short walks early and late are usually enough. Midday walks should be avoided.

### Can I keep my dog on the balcony?
Only if it is shaded, ventilated, and cool. Direct sun is not safe.

### What if my dog refuses water?
Offer fresh water in a clean bowl and keep it nearby. If refusal continues, contact your vet.

### Is playing indoors okay during summer?
Light play indoors is fine if the room is cool and ventilated.

### Should I change food in summer?
Keep the main diet consistent and avoid sudden changes in hot months.
